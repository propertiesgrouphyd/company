4. CODE OF CONDUCT

Every employee shall:

  • Act honestly and ethically.
  • Treat others with dignity and respect.
  • Protect Company assets.
  • Maintain confidentiality.
  • Avoid conflicts of interest.
  • Comply with Company policies and applicable laws.

5. WORKING HOURS

Working hours shall be communicated by the Company depending on:

  • Department
  • Project Requirements
  • Client Requirements
  • Remote or Office Work

Employees are expected to maintain punctuality and professional discipline.

6. ATTENDANCE

Employees shall:

  • Report attendance accurately.
  • Inform supervisors about absence.
  • Seek approval for leave.
  • Maintain regular attendance.

7. LEAVE POLICY

Employees may avail:

  • Casual Leave
  • Sick Leave
  • Earned Leave
  • Maternity / Paternity Leave
  • Special Leave as approved

Leave entitlement shall be governed by Company policy and applicable laws.

8. CONFIDENTIALITY

Employees shall not disclose:

  • Source Code
  • Business Plans
  • Financial Information
  • Customer Data
  • Trade Secrets
  • Internal Documents

Confidentiality obligations continue even after cessation of employment.

9. COMPANY PROPERTY

Employees shall protect Company property including:

  • Laptops
  • Software
  • Documents
  • Databases
  • Access Credentials
  • Office Equipment

10. INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY USAGE

Employees shall:

  • Use Company systems responsibly.
  • Protect passwords.
  • Avoid unauthorized software.
  • Follow cybersecurity practices.
  • Report security incidents immediately.

11. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

All inventions, software, documents, designs and intellectual property created during employment shall belong exclusively to GIDIGI TECHNOLOGIES PRIVATE LIMITED unless otherwise agreed in writing.

12. ANTI-HARASSMENT

The Company is committed to providing a workplace free from:

  • Harassment
  • Discrimination
  • Bullying
  • Retaliation

Complaints shall be investigated fairly and confidentially.

13. DISCIPLINARY ACTION

Violation of Company policies may result in:

  • Warning
  • Suspension
  • Termination
  • Legal Action where applicable

14. TERMINATION OF EMPLOYMENT

Employment may be terminated:

  • By resignation.
  • By mutual agreement.
  • By retirement.
  • For misconduct.
  • For business reasons.

Notice period and final settlement shall be governed by applicable agreements and laws.

15. GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL

Employees may raise grievances relating to:

  • Workplace Issues
  • Compensation
  • Harassment
  • Discrimination
  • Policy Violations

All grievances shall be handled fairly and confidentially.

16. AMENDMENTS

The Company reserves the right to amend this Handbook from time to time.

The latest approved version shall be treated as the official version.
